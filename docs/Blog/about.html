About Me

I'm a first-generation American, and originally from Ciudad Acuña in northern Mexico, which is not very well-known except for director Robert Rodriguez's El Mariachi, Desperado, and From Dusk Till Dawn films being filmed on site. I learned English at a very young age by memorizing almost all of The Beatles lyrics, and practiced it at least weekly when we visited family members in neighboring Del Rio, TX. In 2001, I received my Legal Residency, and in 2006 I was finally naturalized as an American Citizen.

Swiftly thereafter, I joined the United States Army as a Petroleum Laboratory Specialist, but I soon became "The Tech Guy" because I was always interested in computers and communications systems. Luckily for me, the Army would quickly place me, and my skills, where most needed. From patching my senior manager's computer software to performing data entry and database management, my role became much more as that of a Technical Services Specialist, and I thoroughly enjoyed it.

I was stationed in Fort Lee, VA were I met my wonderful wife (she was also in the US Army). We had our son Xavier in July 2009 in Prince George county. Our next duty station (assigned military base) took us around the world to South Korea. We were stationed in Camp Humprheys, which is about 40 miles south of South Korea's capital, Seoul. Our new daughter Annabelle was born on Jun 2011. The very next year, we were stationed in San Antonio, TX in Fort Sam Houston, which is where I was medically retired on April of 2013. All-in-all I thoroughly loved the military. It allowed us to have wonderful experiences, and to meet some of the most closests of friends.
